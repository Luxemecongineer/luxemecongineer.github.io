Hello! I am Sen Lu, an Assistant Professor in the School of Insurance at the Central University of Finance and Economics. My research lies at the intersection of empirical industrial organization, applied microeconometrics, and game theory, with two main strands. The first studies market structure and strategic interaction using structural econometric methods. The second develops game-theoretic foundations for analyzing behavior under incomplete or latent information. Across these strands, I work on model settings including entry games and matching markets, with applications to insurance demand, spectrum-license mergers, cooperative pharmaceutical R&D, and the thoroughbred breeding market. I received my Ph.D. in Economics from Rice University in 2024.
