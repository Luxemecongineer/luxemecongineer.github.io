Hello! I am Sen Lu, an Assistant Professor in the School of Insurance at the Central University of Finance and Economics. My research studies strategic behavior, identification, and prediction under latent or incomplete information, combining game theory and econometric methods.

One part of my work develops game-theoretic foundations for symmetric environments with latent information, including reduced-form equilibrium characterizations and counterfactual analysis. Another part studies market structure and strategic interaction using structural econometric models. Across these strands, I work on settings including entry games and matching markets, with applications to insurance demand, spectrum-license mergers, cooperative pharmaceutical R&D, and the thoroughbred breeding market.

I received my Ph.D. in Economics from Rice University in 2024.
